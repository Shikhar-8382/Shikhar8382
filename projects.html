Pose Estimation

Pose estimation using machine learning is a computer vision technique that involves predicting the spatial positions of key body joints or landmarks in a given image or video. This process enables machines to understand and interpret the human body's posture and movement. Machine learning models, particularly deep neural networks, are trained on large datasets containing annotated examples of human poses. These models learn to recognize patterns and relationships between image features and corresponding joint locations. Once trained, the models can accurately estimate the pose of a person in real-time by analyzing input from cameras or other sensors. Pose estimation has applications in various fields, such as human-computer interaction, sports analysis, augmented reality, and healthcare, where precise knowledge of body movements is essential for tasks like motion analysis, gesture recognition, and rehabilitation exercises.
